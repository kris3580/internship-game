You mean a special “probability ball” that physically hits normal balls, and the ball it touches changes that number’s spawn weight.

Example:

Green modifier ball hits a 7
→ 7 spawn weight +1
Red modifier ball hits a 4
→ 4 spawn weight -1

That is much better than a menu slider because the probability control is part of the actual physics game.

Why it works

It turns probability control into a skill shot.

Instead of:

click +1 on 7

the player has to:

aim the probability ball so it hits a 7

That creates a very satisfying connection between the board state and the probability system.

Possible modifier balls

Boost ball

Hit a number → that number becomes more common

Suppress ball

Hit a number → that number becomes less common

Swap ball

First hit decreases, second hit increases

Lockout ball

Hit a number → that number cannot appear for the next 5 drops

Favorite ball

Hit a number → that number gets boosted until its next island clears
Balance idea

For your first prototype, I’d do:

Green ball:
+1 weight to the first numbered ball it touches

Red ball:
-1 weight to the first numbered ball it touches
minimum weight = 1

Do not allow weight to go to 0 at first. A minimum of 1 keeps the game from becoming too solved.

Even better

Make the modifier ball itself still fall into the container and take space until used.

So the player has a decision:

Do I use this boost ball now, or is it clogging my board?

That makes it part of survival, not just a free menu action.

This is a strong mechanic. It makes your “probability manipulation” feel physical, readable, and unique.
